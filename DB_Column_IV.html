Behistun Inscription (DB): Column IV

This is a translation and transliteration of the Old Persian version of Column IV of the Behistun Inscription of Darius I.

Translation

Says Darius the king: this is what was done by me in Babylon.
Says Darius the king: this is what I have done by the greatness of Ahuramazda in a single year after I became king. I fought nineteen battles. By the greatness of Ahuramazda I struck down and captured nine kings.

One magus named Gaumata lied thus: he said “I am Bardiya, the son of Cyrus”. He made Persia rebellious.
One Elamite named Açina lied thus: he said “I am king in Elam”. He made Elam rebellious to me.
One Babylonian named Nidintu-Bel lied thus: he said “I am Nebuchadnezzar, the son of Nabonidus”. He made Babylonia rebellious.
One Persian named Martiya lied thus: he said “I am Imaniš, king in Elam”. He made Elam rebellious.
One Median named Phraortes lied thus: he said “I am Xšathrita of the family of Uvaxšatra”. He made Media rebellious.
One Sagartian named Ciçataxma lied thus: he said “I am king in Sagartia, of the family of Uvaxšatra”. He made Sagartia rebellious.
One Margian named FrÄda lied thus: he said “I am king in Margiana”. He made Margiana rebellious.
One Persian named VahyazdÄta lied thus: he said “I am Bardiya, the son of Cyrus”. He made Persia rebellious.
One Armenian named Araxa lied thus: he said “I am Nebuchadnezzar, the son of Nabonidus”. He made Babylon rebellious.

Says Darius the king: I captured these nine kings amidst these battles.
Says Darius the king: these lands which became rebellious, the Lie made them rebellious; they lied to the people, then Ahuramazda made them over into my hand. According to my wish, I made them thus.
Says Darius the king: you who shall be king hereafter, stand strongly against the Lie. The man who is a follower of the Lie, punish him, if you shall think “may my land be secure”.

Says Darius the king: this is what I have done by the greatness of Ahuramazda in a single year. You who shall hereafter read this inscription, believe what I have done; you shall not think it to be a lie.
Says Darius the king: I swear by Ahuramazda that it is true and not a lie that I have done this in a single year.
Says Darius the king: by the greatness of Ahuramazda there is much else I have done that is not written in this inscription. For this reason it is not written: so that whoever shall hereafter read this inscription shall not think it too much, and not believe this that I have done, and think it to be a lie.

Says Darius the king: by those who were formerly kings was not done so much as what I did by the greatness of Ahuramazda in a single year.
Says Darius the king: now, believe what I have done in that matter. You shall not hide it, on account of the people. If you shall not hide this testimony but proclaim it to the people, may Ahuramazda be pleased with you, and may your family be great, and may you live long.
Says Darius the king: if you hide this testimony and do not proclaim it to the people, may Ahuramazda strike you down, and may your family end.

Says Darius the king: this is what I have done in a single year. By the greatness of Ahuramazda I did it. Ahuramazda brought me aid, and the other gods who are.
Says Darius the king: for the following reason Ahuramazda bore me aid, and the other gods who are. For I was not disloyal, nor was I a follower of the Lie, nor was I an evildoer, neither I nor my family. By the right, I abided.
I did wrong neither to the weak nor to the strong. The man who worked hard in my house, him I rewarded; him who did harm, I punished.

Says Darius the king: you who shall be king hereafter, the man who shall be a follower of the Lie or the man who shall be a wrongdoer, do not be pleased with them; punish them.
Says Darius the king: you who shall hereafter see this inscription that I wrote or these images, you shall not destroy them but preserve them for as long as you live.
Says Darius the king: if you see this inscription or these images and shall not destroy them and you will preserve them for as long as your family may live, may Ahuramazda be greatly pleased with you and your family as long as you live, and that which you shall do, may Ahuramazda make it easy for you.
Says Darius the king: if you shall see this inscription or these images and you shall destroy them, and you will not preserve them as long as your family may live, may Ahuramazda strike you down and your family end, and that which you shall do, may Ahuramazda destroy.

Says Darius the king: these are the men who were there at that time when I killed the magus Gaumata, who called himself Bardiya. At that time these men worked hard and were my loyal followers:
VidafranÄ, the son of VÄyaspÄra, a Persian;
UtÄna, the son of Thušra, a Persian;
Gaubaruva, the son of Taraduniya, a Persian;
Vidarna, the son of BagÄbigana, a Persian;
Bagabuxša, the son of DÄtuvahaya, a Persian;
Ardumniš, the son of Vahuka, a Persian.

Says Darius the king: you who shall be king hereafter, hold in good favour the families of these men.
Says Darius the king: by the greatness of Ahuramazda I made this inscription of mine. In addition, it was Aryan, and it was written both on tablet and on parchment. In addition I made an image of myself; in addition I set out my lineage. It was both written and read before me.
Afterwards I sent out this inscription everywhere among the lands. The people worked hard…
