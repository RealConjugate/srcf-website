XPh Inscription

This is a translation and transliteration of the Old Persian version of the XPh inscription ("daiva inscription") of Xerxes I at Persepolis.

Translation

A great god is Ahuramazda, who put in place this earth, who put in place that sky, who put in place man, who put in place happiness for man, who made Xerxes king, one king of many, one commander of many.

I am Xerxes, great king, king of kings, king of lands of many kinds of people, king in this great earth far and wide, son of Darius the king, an Achaemenid, a Persian, son of a Persian, an Aryan, of Aryan stock.

Says Xerxes the king: by the greatness of Ahuramazda, these are the lands that I was king over further away from Persia; I ruled over them; they brought me tribute; what was said to them by me, that they did; the law that was mine, that held them: Media, Elam, Arachosia, Armenia, Drangiana, Parthia, Aria, Bactria, Sogdia, Chorasmia, Babylonia, Assyria, Sattagydia, Lydia, Egypt, the Ionians that live by the sea and those that live across the sea, the Makranians, Arabia, Gandhara, India, Cappadocia, the Dahae, the haoma-drinking Scythians, the Scythians with pointed hats, the Skudrans, the mountain-dwellers, the Libyans, the Carians, and the Ethiopians.

Says Xerxes the king: when I became king, there was, among these lands that are written above, one that was in turmoil. Then Ahuramazda brought me aid; by the greatness of Ahuramazda I struck down that land and put it in its place. And among these lands there was one where previously daivas were worshipped. Afterwards, by the greatness of Ahuramazda, I destroyed that daiva-sanctuary and I declared “may the daivas not be worshipped”. Where previously daivas were worshipped, there I worshipped Ahuramazda artācā brazmaniy. And there were other things that were badly done; that I made good. This that I have done, I did all by the greatness of Ahuramazda. Ahuramazda brought me aid until I did the work.

You who may live hereafter, if you should think “I shall be šiyāta when living and I shall be artāvā when dead”, obey that law that Ahuramazda has set down. May you worship Ahuramazda artācā brazmaniy. The man who acts in accordance with that law Ahuramazda has set down, and worships Ahuramazda artācā brazmaniy, he both becomes šiyāta when living and becomes artāvā when dead. Says Xerxes the king: may Ahuramazda protect me against harm, and my house, and this land. This I ask of Ahuramazda; this may Ahuramazda give to me.
